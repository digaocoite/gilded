Our Story

The origins of a traveling private-event series.

Origin

Julian Vale & The Concept

The Gilded Room began as the idea of Julian Vale, a former event stylist and hospitality consultant who became interested in why memorable evenings are often connected to temporary experiences rather than permanent venues.

After years of helping restaurants, galleries, and boutique hotels create private events, Julian imagined a gathering that could move between cities. Each edition would temporarily transform an overlooked or unexpected location and give guests a new theme, atmosphere, dress code, and entry ritual.

The first informal Gilded Room evening was organized for a small group of friends in a restored warehouse loft. Guests received printed invitations without being told exactly what the interior would look like. The evening combined formal clothing, candlelight, a carefully selected playlist, and a password at the door.

The response inspired a traveling series. Today, The Gilded Room appears periodically in different cities. Some evenings are glamorous, some playful, and others inspired by cinema, art, travel, or a particular decade. No two editions are intended to be identical.

“The room is only half the experience. The guests create the other half.”
— Julian Vale, Founder and Creative Director
Pillars

Our Core Ideals

The Gilded Room is built upon three concepts that guide every transformation and gathering.

Transformation

Ordinary or unexpected spaces become temporary worlds for one evening. Through meticulous design, lighting, and layout, we reveal the hidden potential of everyday spaces.

Participation

Guests help create the atmosphere through clothing, conversation, and interpretation of the theme. The crowd is the decor, and the dress code is the design.

Impermanence

Each edition exists briefly and then disappears, leaving behind stories rather than a permanent venue. We build temporary spaces that exist for a few hours and vanish.
