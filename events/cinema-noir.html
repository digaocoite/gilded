Cinema Noir

Des Moines, Iowa

← Back to Previous Evenings

The Evening Overview

In April, The Gilded Room arrived in Des Moines, Iowa, transforming a sprawling, raw photography studio into a shadowed cinematic set piece. The night was heavily influenced by classic mysteries, monochrome fashion, sharp silhouettes, and vintage typography.

Atmospheric projections of classic title cards and slowly moving rain-streaked windowpane shadows were cast onto the whitewashed studio walls. Jazz saxophones and dark ambient piano notes echoed through the brick halls, while brass accents and low spotlights illuminated key gathering areas.

Guest Engagement & Attire

A limited group of invited guests were asked to dress in black, white, silver, and vintage-inspired evening wear. Elegant silk dresses, white dinner jackets, fedoras, and dark sunglasses filled the room, making everyone feel like they were a character stepping off a classic Hollywood film reel.

The Signature Detail

At the door, guests were asked for the password and handed a mini-dossier along with their seating assignment. Inside was a card containing a fictional character name and a short backstory description (e.g., "The Disgraced Journalist," "The Silent Heiress"). Guests were encouraged to use these identities when introducing themselves to others, sparking playful, improvisational theater that animated the entire room.
