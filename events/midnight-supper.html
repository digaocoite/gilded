The Midnight Supper

Omaha, Nebraska

← Back to Previous Evenings

The Evening Overview

In March, The Gilded Room traveled to Omaha, Nebraska, for an exercise in late-night restraint. Taking place inside a historic dining hall with high timber arches, the evening was styled around the quiet, formal elegance of early 20th-century hotel dining rooms and monochrome photography.

The ambient environment was stripped of modern bright colors, emphasizing shadow, polished silver, white tablecloths, and stark black dinnerware. Small lamps placed at each seat provided the primary illumination, casting clean pools of light across the tables while leaving the perimeter of the room in darkness.

Guest Engagement & Attire

To match the black-and-white editorial design, a limited group of invited guests arrived in classic formal evening wear (tuxedos, white shirts, floor-length gowns) but were asked to include one unexpected detail—a flash of unusual jewelry, asymmetrical tailoring, or vintage accessories that broke the strict uniformity of the room.

The Signature Detail

Time played an integral role in the supper's choreography. The grand father clocks placed in the corners of the dining hall had their mechanisms altered so their hands remained frozen at five minutes to midnight. Dinner was served in quiet candlelight until, precisely as the clocks were released and chimed midnight, a single amber spotlight illuminated the center table and dessert was served.
