Policy

Accessibility Statement

Guests with accessibility-related questions are encouraged to contact the event organizer or the person who issued the invitation before attending. Because The Gilded Room uses temporary locations, accessibility features can differ between editions.

We are committed to making our temporary experiences as welcoming and accessible as possible. Because we operate out of diverse environments (such as historic dining halls, private lofts, warehouse settings, and glass-dome conservatories), structural limitations exist that are unique to each venue:

  • Venue Variation: Modern buildings like rooftop conservatories are fully ramp-accessible, whereas historic dining halls or older loft installations may present structural stairs or narrow access ways.
  • Advance Consultation: By coordinating with us before the event date, we can arrange specific assistance, elevator keycodes, or alternative drop-off instructions to facilitate entry.
  • Visual & Auditory Environment: The Gilded Room features candlelit low-lighting and ambient soundtracks. If you require accommodations related to lighting, sound levels, or visual displays, please notify the organizers in advance.

Please use our contact form and select "Accessibility question" as your reason for contact. Our staging team will reach out with the parameters of the specific venue.
